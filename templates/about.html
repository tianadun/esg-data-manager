Purpose

ESG Data Manager is a prototype web application for organising sustainability reporting data. It is designed to help a company collect, structure, review, and export sustainability datapoints and greenhouse gas emissions records in a simple reporting workflow.

The application focuses on the practical data-management side of sustainability reporting: keeping records structured, tracking their status, documenting data sources and evidence links, and preparing information for further analysis or reporting work.

How to use the app

After registration and login, the dashboard gives an overview of the selected company’s sustainability dataset. It shows total calculated emissions in tCO2e, emissions by scope, the largest emissions category, reporting-readiness indicators, dataset size, and status summaries.

The Datapoints module is used for sustainability metrics such as energy, water, waste, financial, workforce, or other ESRS-relevant operational datapoints. Each datapoint can include a reporting year, period, topic, metric name, value, unit, department, data source, evidence link, status, and notes.

The Emissions module is used for greenhouse gas emissions records. Users enter activity data and an emission factor, and the app calculates emissions and displays them in tCO2e. Emissions can be classified by Scope 1, Scope 2, or Scope 3 and by emissions category.

The Quality Check page lists records that may need attention before reporting, such as records with missing evidence links, missing data sources, missing emission factor sources, or statuses marked as Missing or Needs review.

The Export page allows users to download the full datapoints and emissions datasets as CSV files for further work in tools such as Excel, Numbers, Power BI, or other reporting systems.

What the prototype includes

  • User registration, login, and company-specific records.
  • A dashboard for emissions and reporting-readiness overview.
  • A sustainability datapoints module with add, edit, delete, and filter functions.
  • An emissions module with add, edit, delete, filter, and automatic calculation functions.
  • A combined Quality Check page for identifying records that need attention.
  • CSV export for datapoints and emissions records.

Important limitations

This application is an educational prototype, not a complete CSRD compliance platform, certified carbon accounting tool, or external assurance system. It supports structured data collection and review, but it does not determine whether a company fully complies with any legal reporting requirement.

Emission factors are entered manually by the user. The app does not currently include an automatic emission factor database, supplier-specific factor library, or external API connection. Users are responsible for choosing appropriate emission factors and ensuring that activity units match emission factor units.

Scope 3 coverage is simplified. The app includes selected emissions categories relevant for a prototype, but it does not implement the full complexity of Scope 3 accounting, supplier data collection, product-level lifecycle assessment, or complete value-chain modelling.

Evidence links and data sources are stored as text references. The prototype does not upload or manage source documents directly and does not include a full audit trail or approval workflow.

Possible future development

  • User roles, reviewer permissions, and approval workflows.
  • Audit trail showing who changed records and when.
  • CSV import and validation of uploaded datasets.
  • Emission factor library or integration with external factor databases.
  • Charts, trend analysis, and year-on-year comparisons.
  • More advanced Scope 3 categories and supplier-data workflows.
  • Integration with Power BI or other reporting and analytics tools.
